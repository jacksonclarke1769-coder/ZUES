## Column definitions (same DEC-20260706-1108 count-basis discipline as `06_honest_sizing_matrix.md`)

- `n_starts`: eligible monthly-rolling starts with >=12 months forward runway (49, fixed by the
  data span for every cell — this grid varies sizing, not the eval config, so `n_starts` does not
  shrink the way eval `eligible_starts` does with restrictive filters).
- `e_paid` / `med_paid`: mean / median lifetime $ paid out across all 49 starts (BUST starts
  contribute $0 or partial-ladder $ paid before bust; DATA_END starts contribute whatever was paid
  before the data ran out — right-censored, not "failed").
- `med_months`: median months-to-terminal (BUST, CLOSED_MAX, or DATA_END) across ALL 49 starts,
  same convention as `apex_funded_40.main()`'s own `med_months` — DATA_END starts are
  right-censored at the last available data day, which pulls this figure toward the data-end
  boundary for cells that rarely terminate early (see caveat below).
- `bust_pct` / `closed_max_pct` / `data_end_pct`: 100 x count/49 for each terminal status. **"bust %
  observed in model"** — not a guaranteed probability (see overlap caveat).
- `safety_net_pct`: % of starts whose EOD peak balance ever reached `LOCK_EOD=$52,600` (the point
  the trailing threshold locks at start+$100 and can no longer bust from a give-back below start).
- `med_days_to_safety_net`: median day-offset among starts that reached it (blank/0 if none did).
- `worst_day`: minimum single-day realized $ across the cell's full `daily_series` (a property of
  the day-level P&L stream at that budget/cap, independent of PA start index) — floor is exactly
  `-$1,000` once the Apex DLL trough-clamp binds.
- `payout_count_dist_pct`: % of the 49 starts terminating having banked exactly `k` payouts
  (`k=0..6`; `k=6` == `CLOSED_MAX`, the ladder fully paid out and the PA closed).
